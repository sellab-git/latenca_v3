# Market scan — Redbubble · Juniqe · Andy okay (breadth, 2026-07-24)

**Purpose:** understand the wall-art market from more angles than the 3 core competitors (Mixtiles/iamfy/Displate). Driven live (Playwright). Screenshots local only. Each = a DIFFERENT business model; the value is the contrast + the reusable conversion patterns, especially on the **single-piece PDP** (our priority path — memory `single-piece-is-primary-path`).

## Redbubble — uncurated UGC marketplace, "design on everything"
`redbubble.com` framed-print PDP (`/i/framed-print/...`). Model: **one design → 60+ products** ("View this design on +60 products" — same art on framed print / poster / canvas / **and** t-shirts, stickers, phone cases, mugs, pillows…). Wall art is ONE category among many.
- **PDP pickers:** Size (Small 8×8" / Medium 12×12" / Large 16×16" — inches, US) · **Frame Style** (Box Frame / Flat Frame) · frame colour · "View size guide". Framed print here = $152.84 (pricey).
- **Light on per-product social proof** (no rating/review block on the PDP) — scale over curation; anyone uploads.
- **Contrast for us:** the anti-curation extreme. Massive catalog, zero guidance, art diluted across merch. Latenca is the opposite: curated, wall-art-focused, advisor-guided. Take nothing structural; it's the "what not to be" pole.

## Juniqe — curated European brand, framing + premium materials
`juniqe.com` poster PDP. Model: **curated, tasteful** (vs Redbubble's UGC), still design-on-products but design-forward. Distinctive = **material & framing depth**: premium substrates (Hahnemühle fine-art paper, **Alu-Dibond**, **Acrylic glass**, Forex plate) + **many frame types/options** ("Without Frame / More Frames / More frame types & options available"). "Change product" swaps the design onto another product type.
- **Pricing:** accessible tiers £5.95–£26.95 by size. **Social proof: 5,355 reviews.**
- **Take:** premium **material/substrate options** as a quality/price ladder; framing as a first-class choice; curated aesthetic + review social proof. Relevant to our material/frame pickers (map to Gelato substrates).

## Andy okay — cause-driven curated boutique (richest pattern set) ★
`andyokay.com` — "Art for Causes™": limited works by award-winning artists, **part of proceeds to charity**. Small brand, differentiates on **meaning + curation + social proof + urgency**, not scale/tech.
- **PDP (`/products/you-got-this`):** **CHOOSE TYPE** (Fine Art Print / Premium Canvas) · **CHOOSE SIZE** M 12×16" / L 20×28" / XL 28×40" / GIANT 40×55", each **regular→sale** ($190→$59) **with decision-guidance labels "Most Popular" / "Best Value"** · **"(Frames added in the next step)"** = progressive config · **CHOOSE DONATION** — buyer picks the charity (WWF / Rainforest Trust / Non-Violence Project / Shared Impact Fund / $0).
- **Conversion stack:** heavy social proof (**202,000 Collectors · 13,020 reviews · 226 signed artists · 12 charity partners**) + urgency/value (**Buy 2 Get 1 Free**, "ALMOST GONE", limited "Atelier Exclusive", big % off) + geo free-shipping.
- **Take (strong, directly usable for a curated brand):**
  - **Decision-guidance labels on options** ("Most Popular" / "Best Value") — literal "sell confidence, not choice"; nudge the size/option pick, cut paralysis.
  - **Progressive config** — keep the single-piece PDP simple; upsell frame in a *next step* (fits our priority: N=1 path must be clean).
  - **Meaning/story** (artist + cause) as differentiation for a curated shop.
  - Social proof + tasteful urgency as the core conversion levers of a small curated brand (no composer needed).

## Cross-cutting market truths (now 6 sources: + Mixtiles/iamfy/Displate)
1. **The core unit is the SINGLE piece.** Every player's spine = a PDP with **material / size (/ frame)** pickers. Confirms single-piece is the priority path.
2. **Nobody does curated asymmetric composition.** Multi-piece is driven by **quantity discounts / BOGO / "frequently bought together"**, never a designed layout → still Latenca's white space (as an optional layer, not the common case).
3. **Guest-first checkout is universal** (Displate own, iamfy Shopify, all guest) → our auth stance holds.
4. **Conversion levers are consistent:** social proof (reviews/collectors), tasteful urgency (countdown/BOGO/limited), **decision-guidance labels**, cross-sell/volume, geo delivery, progressive config. These — not a wall builder — carry most single-piece conversions.
5. **Curation + meaning beat scale** for a boutique (Andy okay vs Redbubble): Latenca should lean on curation, artist story, and (optionally) a cause/impact angle — cheap, differentiating, on-brand.

## Distinctive patterns worth stealing (shortlist for the spec)
- Decision-guidance labels ("Most Popular"/"Best Value") on size/options (Andy okay).
- Progressive config — frame/extras in a later step, PDP stays simple (Andy okay).
- Premium material/substrate ladder as a price/quality choice (Juniqe → Gelato variants).
- Social proof + tasteful urgency + cross-sell as the single-piece conversion stack (all).
- (Uploads, if ever) AI upscaler + explicit rights gate (Displate Custom).
